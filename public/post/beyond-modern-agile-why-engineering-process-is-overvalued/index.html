Beyond Modern Agile - Why Engineering Process Is Overvalued

Jun 7, 2018

Many engineers and managers focus too much on process and overlook much more important aspects of a successful engineering team.

I’ve spent this year interviewing a lot of people. One question that inevitably comes up: “What’s your engineering process, are you an agile team?”. The terms agile, scrum, sprint are thrown around rather loosely and from my experience I would argue that we barely have a shared understanding of what they mean. While that’s a favorite pet peeve of mine, its besides the point of this post.

Process is a focal point when it comes to building engineering teams. In my opinion though it is one of the least important aspects. People gravitate towards it because it is tangible and easy.

Why process matters

The most important goal of modern agile processes are to “keep the trains running on time” and to develop product iteratively.

How did we get here?

What is the stuff that really matters?

Engineers should know what to work on and why they are working on it. They should have a point person for each of their dependencies.

  • The ratio of junior to senior engineers on the team. This is important for both development of team members and for velocity of the team.
  • Engineers are bought into the prioritized initiatives because they participated in planning and have a good undertanding of the customers they are building product for
  • The team has momentum. It’s shipping valuable improvements at a frequent rate. The results of their efforts are visible in metrics and shared with the team frequently.
  • Team chemistry & identity, time to bond and get to know each other
  • Room for creativity
Process
  • There’s a clear list of prioritized initiatives. At any point in time engineers on the team know what their next tasks should be (agile speak for this is “prioritized backlog”)
  • Each major initiative needs an owner that can be held accountable.
  • Initiatives that have dependencies (such as design work, research, etc.) should have a point person for each dependency

Timelines

  • The best way to deliver stuff on time is to keep initiatives and deliverables as small as possible. This doesn’t require hours of estimation meetings.
